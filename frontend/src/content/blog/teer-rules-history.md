The history of Teer dates back decades, deeply intertwined with the Khasi culture of Meghalaya. What started as local archery competitions evolved into a structured, daily event.

## History of Teer

Archery has always been more than a sport for the Khasi people; it's a martial tradition and a social event. Historically, villages would organize archery competitions, and informal betting on the outcomes naturally followed.

In 1982, the Meghalaya government formalized the game by passing the Meghalaya Amusement and Betting Tax Act. This move provided a legal framework, generated state revenue, and solidified the Khasi Hills Archery Sports Association (KHASA) as the organizing body.

## The Rules of the Game

1. **The Target**: The 'Teer' target is composed of straw or bamboo. It must be of a specific size and placed at a regulated distance from the archers.
2. **The Archers**: Fifty archers from different archery clubs participate in each session. They must use traditional wooden bows and arrows. No modern compound bows are allowed.
3. **The Shoot**:
   - **Round 1 (3:45 PM)**: Archers shoot a maximum of 30 arrows each over a duration of a few minutes.
   - **Round 2 (4:45 PM)**: The number of arrows is reduced to 20 per archer.
4. **The Count**: After the shooting stops, officials and club representatives gather around the target. They carefully count out loud the number of arrows that hit.
5. **The Result**: The winning two-digit number is derived from the last two digits of the total count. If 872 arrows hit in Round 1, the result is "72".

## Fair Play

To ensure fairness, the entire counting process is public. The results are instantly transmitted across betting counters in the state. Transparency is critical to maintaining trust in the daily draws.
