Shillong Teer is a unique and legal lottery game played in Meghalaya, India. Unlike regular lotteries that rely on drawn numbers, Teer results are directly based on the number of arrows shot during an archery session.

## The Tradition

Archery is a deeply rooted tradition in Meghalaya. The Khasi Hills Archery Sports Association (KHASA) organizes the daily Teer events. It is a source of daily engagement for thousands and an important part of the local culture.

## How It Works

1. **The Setup**: There are two rounds played daily from Monday to Saturday at the Polo Ground in Shillong.
2. **The Archery**: In each round, 50 archers shoot arrows at a target made of grass/bamboo. They shoot up to 30 arrows in the first round and 20 in the second round.
3. **The Result**: Officials count the number of arrows that hit the target. The last two digits of the total number of arrows hitting the target form the winning number.

*For example, if 1,045 arrows hit the target in Round 1, the winning number for Round 1 is **45**.*

## Is it Legal?

Yes, Shillong Teer is completely legal in the state of Meghalaya, regulated under the Meghalaya Amusement and Betting Tax Act. It provides employment and supports the local archery association.

## Why Do People Play?

It's a mix of tradition, community gathering, and the thrill of prediction. Many players base their numbers on their dreams from the previous night—a fascinating cultural phenomenon unique to Teer.
